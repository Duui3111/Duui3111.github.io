# Example from https://qiskit.org/documentation/tutorials/circuits/1_getting_started_with_qiskit.html
from qiskit import *

IBMQ.save_account('MY_API_TOKEN')

# Create a Quantum Circuit acting on a quantum register of three qubits
circ=QuantumCircuit(3)

# Add a H gate on qubit 0, putting this qubit in superposition.
circ.h(0)

# Add a CX (CNOT) gate on control qubit 0 and target qubit 1, putting
# the qubits in a Bell state.
circ.cx(0, 1)

# Add a CX (CNOT) gate on control qubit 0 and target qubit 2, putting
# the qubits in a GHZ state.
circ.cx(0, 2)

# displays the version of qiskit
print("IBM Qiskit: ", qiskit.__qiskit_version__)

# displays the circuit graph
circ.draw('mpl')


ABOUT QUANTUM COMPUTING

Quantum Computing is a fast computer that does calculations that run true and false (0 and 1 both run together). A quantum computer uses qubits or quantum bits to compute algorithms. Inside each qubit there are 0 and 1 linked together. Superposition and entanglement are important features of quantum computers. The 0 and 1 can occur at the same time and at different levels, which is what superposition is. Entanglement is also important for quantum computers because it describes the relationship and interaction between the entities. This concept allows the quantum computer to operate large quantities of data at the same time. They were made to run harder algorithms than regular computers. For example a classical computer will take 1 week for it to solve a problem, but a quantum computer will take 1 second. [0][2][4][6]


HISTORY OF QUANTUM COMPUTING

Richard Faynman, Paul Benioff and Yuri Manin in 1981 came up with a model of a quantum computer. In 1994 Peter Shor created the Shor algorithm that helped invent the quantum computer. This algorithm could solve problems that were hard for classical computers. The first tiny quantum computer was created in 1998 by Isaac Chuang of the Los Alamos National Laboratory, Neil Gershenfeld of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and Mark Kubinec of the University of California at Berkeley. It was a 2-qubit computer and was able to solve many algorithms. D-wave was a Canadian company that created the first quantum computer with 28 qubits in 2007. It was the take-off of the quantum computer. Many companies are in competition with one another to create the first powerful quantum computer. Some companies include IBM, Google and Microsoft. Many companies are spending billion of dollars on research and development. Quantum computers are not as powerful as what they are intended for, but are expected to get there in the next 10-20 years. [1][5][8]

WHAT A QUANTUM COMPUTER IS USED FOR

A quantum computer is used for many applications. Firstly, it is used in chemical and biological engineering. For example, a quantum computer helps with the discovery and manipulation of molecules, creates simulations of chemical reactions, new properties of molecules, and discovers and develop new materials and drugs. Secondly, quantum computing is used in the cybersecurity industry because it has made cracking encryption much easier. in the future, the quantum computer can be used to create artificial intelligence. Also, the quantum computer can make financial calculations. It is also used in complex manufacturing products. Today, they are attempting to build a quantum communication system so they can make a quantum version of the internet. [5][8]
